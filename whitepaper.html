Entropy Press

Whitepaper

Draft 1 — March 13, 2026

We don't press books. We press changes into reality.

A press used to mean a machine that forced ink into paper and fixed an idea in final form. Entropy Press is not that.

We don't care what form your change takes. It might be a book. It might be software. It might be a protocol, a method, a tool, a framework, or something we don't have a word for yet. The form is incidental. The change is what matters.

What we've built is infrastructure for launching things that change people — and for making sure the people who were changed are the ones who set the price.

The Problem

Every launch mechanism in existence imports noise from outside the system.

Token launches give price discovery to speculators who've never used the product. Publishing gives gatekeeping to editors who read on deadline. Product Hunt gives rankings to strangers who arrived via Twitter. App stores give visibility to algorithms optimizing for engagement, not impact.

The people who understand the work are never the people pricing it. The people pricing it are importing entropy — randomness that isn't tied to the builder or the product. That entropy corrupts the signal. The price you get at launch tells you more about market conditions and timing than about whether what you built matters.

This is not a bug that can be fixed with better marketing. It's a structural failure in how launches work.

The Mechanism: Feedback as Bonding Curve

Entropy Press launches work in three stages.

Stage 1 — Release

The creator ships a first piece — a chapter, a prototype, a demo, a proof. Not the whole thing. Enough to generate genuine signal.

Access to the release is gated. You have to sign up to receive it. Signing up is a soft commitment: I believe this might be worth my time.

Stage 2 — Feedback Collection

Every reader gives feedback. Two types, because they measure different things.

Structured feedback is the author's instrument. Predetermined questions tied to the specific claims and intentions of the work. Did this chapter shift your thinking on X? Did you understand Y the way I intended? Did the argument in section Z hold?

Structured feedback proves the work does what it claims. It's measurable, comparable across readers, and signals to the auction market: the thesis holds.

Freeform feedback is the reader's signal. No prompts. No expected answers. Just: what happened to you?

Freeform feedback is unpredictable by design. Someone finishing the work and writing "this made me want to quit my job and actually ship something" is worth more to a prospective buyer than any structured response. It proves resonance — the change actually changed someone. That's the ceiling signal.

Both streams accumulate during the collection period. Together, they form the bonding curve: a provable record of who engaged, how deeply, and what the work did to them.

Stage 3 — Coordinated Auction

When the signal is ready — when the curve has enough depth — the launch fires.

Every person who gave feedback during collection gets coordinated at launch. Not as a random audience, but as the founding network: the people who were there before it had a price. They get first access to the auction. Their feedback statements become the public record of why the thing is worth owning.

The auction isn't separate from the feedback — it's the crystallization of it. The floor is set by the density of structured validation. The ceiling is set by the depth of freeform impact. External buyers can participate, but they're buying into a curve that was already shaped by people who know the work.

The builder retains gravity throughout. They control who gets access to give feedback. They set the threshold at which the auction fires. What they give up is the ability to manipulate the curve — the signal has to be real, because the signal is the curve.

Why This Works

You can only participate in pricing if you participated in validation.

This isn't a rule. It's architectural. The bonding curve is built from feedback. If you didn't give feedback, you have nothing to contribute to the curve. External speculators can show up at the auction, but they're buying into a signal they didn't shape.

Every current launch mechanism imports entropy from outside the system — bots, whales, hype cycles, algorithm timing. This mechanism seals the system before it opens. The only randomness that matters is the randomness generated by real engagement with the actual work. That's not noise. That's signal.

The builder gets a floor that's real. The community gets a price they shaped. The market gets provable demand that predates the auction.

This is builder insurance: you can't launch into a void, because the launch is the community. If nobody shows up to give feedback, there's no auction. If people show up, the creator got a hundred genuine reactions to their work regardless of what the auction does. The floor is never zero.

The Agent Layer

The coordination problem — who gets notified, when, in what order, with what information — is where an agent lives.

Entropy Press runs an agent that watches the feedback accumulate. It scores incoming structured feedback against the author's intent. It weights freeform feedback by resonance signals — length, specificity, emotional register. It tracks the curve shape over time.

When the threshold is reached, the agent fires the launch sequence: notifies the founding network in coordinated sequence, surfaces the highest-signal feedback statements as auction context, and triggers the auction contract.

The agent isn't setting the price. It's protecting the price from being set by the wrong people.

Technical Architecture

Feedback Collection

  • Drip service handles reader signup and content delivery
  • Two feedback endpoints: /structured (JSON schema per work) and /freeform (text)
  • Each response cryptographically attributed to the reader's wallet address
  • Responses hashed and stored on-chain as proof of engagement before auction

Bonding Curve Computation

  • Structured feedback scored against author-defined rubric (0–1 per question)
  • Freeform feedback scored by signal agent (resonance, specificity, length)
  • Curve value = Σ(structured scores × weight) + Σ(freeform scores × weight) / total_readers
  • Curve is public, real-time, auditable

Auction Layer

  • rare-cli deploys auction contract per work
  • Founding reader NFTs minted at feedback submission (proof of participation)
  • Auction opens when curve crosses author-set threshold
  • Founding network gets priority access window before public
  • Feedback statements attached as on-chain metadata to auction lot

Coordination Agent

  • Monitors curve in real-time
  • Fires launch sequence on threshold crossing
  • Manages notification sequencing for founding network
  • Surfaces signal-ranked feedback during auction window
  • Funds its own inference from auction fees
